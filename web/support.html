Getting started

Copy things the way you always do with ⌘C. Pastry quietly records text and images as you go. When you need something back, press ⌘⇧V to open the history panel near your cursor, use ↑↓ to choose an item, and press ↩ to paste it into whatever app you were using — or just click it.

Frequently asked questions

Why does Pastry ask for Accessibility access?
Accessibility permission lets Pastry synthesize the single ⌘V keystroke that pastes your chosen item back into the app you were using. If you decline, Pastry still works — it copies the item to your clipboard and you press ⌘V yourself. To enable it later, open the menu bar icon → Enable Auto-Paste…, or System Settings → Privacy & Security → Accessibility.
Where is my clipboard history stored?
In a single file on your Mac at ~/Library/Application Support/Pastry/history.json. Nothing is uploaded anywhere.
How much history does Pastry keep?
The 25 most recent items, matching Windows' clipboard history. Pinned items don't count toward that limit and never expire. Items larger than 4 MB are skipped.
How do I pin, delete, or clear items?
With an item selected, press P to pin or unpin and ⌫ to delete. “Clear All” in the panel removes everything except your pinned items. The menu bar icon also has a Clear History option.
Does Pastry capture passwords?
No. Pastry honors the standard macOS markers that password managers and other apps use to flag content as concealed or transient, and skips those items.
Can Pastry launch automatically?
Yes — open the menu bar icon and toggle Start at Login.
How do I uninstall it?
Quit Pastry from the menu bar, move the app to the Trash, and optionally delete the ~/Library/Application Support/Pastry folder.
What are the system requirements?
macOS 13 (Ventura) or later.
