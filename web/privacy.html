Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 22, 2026

The short version: Pastry stores your clipboard history locally on your device. No data is collected, transmitted, or shared.

What Pastry stores

To do its job, Pastry keeps a history of items you copy — text and images — so you can paste them again later. This history is written to a single file on your own Mac at ~/Library/Application Support/Pastry/history.json. It stays on your device.

What Pastry does not do

  • It does not collect, transmit, sell, or share your data.
  • It has no accounts, no sign-in, and no sync server.
  • It contains no analytics, tracking, or advertising SDKs.
  • It makes no network connections with your clipboard contents.

Sensitive content

Pastry respects the standard macOS markers that password managers and other apps use to flag content as concealed or transient, and does not record those items. Items larger than 4 MB are skipped.

Permissions

Pastry may request Accessibility permission solely to synthesize the ⌘V keystroke that pastes your selected item into the app you were using. This permission is used only for that purpose and involves no data collection. If you decline it, Pastry still functions — it copies your selection to the clipboard for you to paste manually.

Your control

You can delete any item, clear your history, or remove the entire ~/Library/Application Support/Pastry folder at any time. Removing the app removes its functionality completely.
